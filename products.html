TIKOG MAT
Crafting Procedure
These handwoven banig mats are crafted from tikog, a reed grass that grows in the swampy fields of the Philippines. The process begins with harvesting the tikog, followed by drying and weaving it into intricate, geometric patterns. The mats are often decorated with colorful strips of buri, adding vibrancy and reflecting cultural and natural motifs. This traditional art form, particularly celebrated in Basey, Samar, showcases the skill and patience of local artisans who turn humble grass into durable, beautiful pieces of Filipino heritage.

TIKOG SLIPPERS
Crafting Procedure
The creation of tikog slippers in Basey, Samar, involves a detailed and skilled process that transforms the raw material into a vibrant, attractive item. The reeds are then cut into various shapes, often featuring intricate, stylish designs. These are dyed in a variety of colors, with combinations chosen to appeal to women’s tastes, the primary market for these slippers. The end result is a unique and durable product that reflects both the craftsmanship and cultural heritage of Basey.

TIKOG BAG
Crafting Procedure
A tikog bag is made from the fibers of tikog, a reed grass found in Basey, Samar. The grass is woven into bags, with colorful patterns embroidered using dyed buri palm leaves. These vibrant designs reflect the community’s deep connection to the land, where local resources support both livelihoods and cultural identity. The bags are functional yet serve as cultural symbols, representing the artistry and traditions of the people of Basey​.

Crafting Procedure
A tikog wallet is a stylish, eco-friendly accessory made from tikog. Traditionally used in crafting banig mats, tikog is woven into intricate patterns, showcasing Filipino craftsmanship. TIKOG FAN
Crafting Procedure
A tikog fan is a traditional Filipino accessory, valued for its blend of practicality and artistry. Made from tikog, the fan showcases the intricate craftsmanship of local artisans, often featuring colorful designs that reflect Filipino cultural heritage. Tikog fans are lightweight, durable, and commonly used for cooling, while also serving as decorative items in cultural events and rituals. Their unique texture and craftsmanship make them a symbol of Filipino creativity and resourcefulness. These fans are a beautiful example of sustainable craftsmanship, combining function with cultural significance​.
